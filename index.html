About Me

2018-current - I'm currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in Dr. Phillip Scott and Dr. Dan Beiting's lab in the Department of Pathobiology at the University of Pennsylvania (UPenn). I investigate big gene expression metadata of experimental models and human patients in American Tegumentary Leishmaniasis. I have been using my Immunology and Cell Biology background to find expression patterns in those datasets that contribute to understanding the immune system in the infections caused by the protozoan Leishmania spp. Additionally, I am a teaching assistant in the DIYtranscriptomics course at UPenn. 2017 - I was awarded a Ph.D. fellow scholarship to learn a new science field abroad. Under Dr. Dan Beiting and Dr. Phillip Scott's supervision at UPenn, I started my journey in Bioinformatics and Computational biology. 2012-2017 - Throughout these years, I developed my Master's and P.hD. degrees in Immunology and Health Sciences under Dr. Edgar M. Carvalho's mentoring in the Hospital of Clinics and Universidade Federal da Bahia. I used cellular biology -wet lab- technics to characterize and understand the role of Innate Immune cells in the infection caused by the Human T-lymphotropic virus type I (HTLV-1). 2011 - I graduated from the Escola Bahiana de Medicina in Salvador-Bahia-Brazil with a. B.S. in Biomedical Sciences. In college, I had my first experience with science, and since then, I knew that research would be a career goal.
